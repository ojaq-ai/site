Each agent has specifications.

Ojaq agents should be understood like products. The point is not abstract capability. The point is a defined agent with a clear profile.

Memory

How much continuity the agent carries, what it remembers, and how well it preserves context.

Intelligence

Its reasoning quality, judgment, and ability to understand what matters in the moment.

Initiative

How actively the agent intervenes, guides, or waits, and how much social confidence it carries.

Voice

Its tone, cadence, and spoken presence when the product needs to act through audio.

Tools

What the agent can access, operate, and orchestrate across workflows and environments.

Form class

Whether the agent begins in software, a still product, or a moving product and how it evolves.

Two form families.

Ojaq starts with a simple split. Still forms bring calm presence into a place. Moving forms carry the same agent identity through space and time.

Still

Room presence

For meeting rooms, classrooms, and shared spaces where the agent becomes a calm layer of coordination.

Still

Desk presence

For focused environments where the agent remains available without taking over the workspace.

Still

Ambient presence

For subtle, always-available interaction where the product fades into the background until needed.

Moving

Personal companion

For movement across the day, carrying continuity, context, and coordination with the user.

Moving

Field mobility

For operational settings where the agent has to move with the work instead of waiting at a desk.

Moving

Physical autonomy

For future products that navigate space while staying minimal, legible, and useful.
